# Outline: The Dossier (Audit as Narrative)

## Structural Concept
**Tell the story as an assembled audit dossier** created after the Canon Vote by an adversarial review team (or the Guardian Foundation’s audit arm). The “chapters” are curated artifacts—transcripts, trace excerpts, private messages, ceremony footage logs—stitched into a readable arc with minimal narrator intrusion.

This outline keeps the core events identical, but changes the reading experience into a **found-document thriller**: the suspense is “what really happened inside the rules,” and the emotional punch comes from what leaks through in supposedly professional artifacts.

## Format Rules (to keep it a novel, not a binder)
- Each chapter is anchored by **one scene artifact** (a transcript, a call recording, a meeting, a ceremony clip) that plays like drama.
- Short “analyst notes” appear only when needed to orient, never to explain theme.
- The dossier’s existence is justified by the world: auditability and reversibility are part of legitimacy in agalmic institutions.

## The Dossier’s Official Purpose
The dossier exists because the network’s charter *requires* auditability and reversal windows for any standard that behaves like cultural canon. After the Canon Vote, an adversarial review is triggered automatically.

The novel’s hook becomes: **“What did the process actually do?”** Not what anyone claims it did.

## Act I — The Packet Opens, and It’s Worse Than Rumor

### Chapter 1: “Recommendation Language” Memo (Operating Company)
An internal memo shows careful wording: “not normative,” “recommended default,” “access and safety.” The memo also contains the Sprint clock: comment windows, rollout dates, and a binding **Canon Vote** scheduled early.

### Chapter 2: Congratulation Flood (Public Feed Extract)
A collage of messages: flattery and soft coercion. A few posts already treat Elena as decree. A single line from Mira stands out: *We need to talk.*

### Chapter 3: Private Call Transcript — Mira & Elena
A full dramatic scene, but as transcript: Mira’s “velvet glove on a fist” argument; Elena’s defensive sincerity; the friendship temperature shift.

### Chapter 4: Echo Anomaly Report #1 (“The Clock”)
Echo flags sequencing effects and intimidation signatures with constrained language: it can show patterns but can’t say “this is illegitimate.” Elena reacts: relief at having proof, dread at what proof can’t do.

**Act I Turn:** The packet makes clear the key fact: the process is “open” in form and closed in practice.

## Act II — The Mechanism of Capture (Within the Rules)

### Chapter 5: Rollout Sequencing Table (Audit Exhibit)
A simple artifact that reads like a thriller reveal: which sanctuaries got which “defaults” first, and how early feedback became “inevitable consensus.”

### Chapter 6: Operating Company Minutes (The Executive’s Sincerity)
The Operator Executive is not a villain. Their minutes are full of care: dissociation incidents, consent failures, accessibility gaps. Their moral blind spot appears as a line: *“We can’t wait for perfect legitimacy; we have to protect people now.”*

### Chapter 7: Viral Critique (Solène’s Post, Unredacted)
Solène’s essay lands as an artifact with heat: canonization as meaning-tranquilizer; comfort becoming governance. The packet includes engagement graphs and the first wave of reputational attacks.

### Chapter 8: “Unscripted Week” Planning Thread
Group messages, vow drafts, logistics—time is the cost. A participant backs out publicly. Another doubles down. The packet captures how a movement forms without central permission.

### Chapter 9: Co-option Attempt (Branding Deck + Internal Email)
The Operating Company tries to incorporate Unscripted Week as proof of “authentic living support.” Solène’s public refusal appears as a clip transcript; Elena’s private response is a short, raw note to Echo: *We’re turning people into props.*

### Chapter 10: Harm Hearing Transcript (The Fight Over Definitions)
Not the whole hearing—just the sections where the combat is clearest: what counts as harm, who gets to define it, and how “access” becomes reputational leverage.

**Act II Turn:** The audit team’s margin note: *The content debate is unwinnable; only ritual and constraint can hold against tempo.*

## Act III — Costly Signals Enter the Record

### Chapter 11: Ceremony Footage Log (The Refusal)
A timestamped clip log of the crowning ceremony. Elena refuses the role on stage and names tempo/reputation/consent friction. The artifact format adds tension: we can see exactly where the room turns.

### Chapter 12: The Pluralism Covenant (Tracked Changes)
The covenant is shown in tracked changes with comments from Elena and Mira. Each “tooth” is argued in margin notes: labeled sanctuary styles, randomized sequencing, reversal windows, high-friction defaults.

### Chapter 13: AI Guardian Trace Excerpt + Adversarial Audit Invite
The AI seat refuses to “decide by model” and opens its deliberation trace for live adversarial review, accepting modification authority if bias is found. This is the machine’s costly signal: vulnerability.

### Chapter 14: Canon Vote Record (Triad Transcript)
We hear the split between the human guardians (legacy precedent fear vs contemporary legitimacy fear), and the procedural moment where pluralism passes narrowly.

### Chapter 15: Elena’s Post-Vote Sanctuary Revision Log
Elena changes “American Tapestry” first to comply with the covenant—making it less coherent, less flattering, less “Elena.” Visitor complaint excerpts show the cost landing in public.

### Ending Image (Final Exhibit): Departures
A short set of “departure statements” from Unscripted Week—simple, human, uneven. Comfort remains available; it can’t pretend to be neutral anymore.

## Why This Structure Is Distinct
- **Nonlinear, artifact-driven suspense**: readers assemble the truth the way auditors do.
- **Natural way to show process mechanics** without turning into essay: charts, clips, minutes, and transcripts are “action.”
- **Fits the world’s ethos**: auditability, reversibility, and legitimacy rituals are built into agalmic institutions.

## Risks
- **Emotional distance**: requires careful selection of artifacts that still feel like scenes with desire, fear, and reversal.
- **Reader fatigue**: the packet must stay lean; each artifact needs a narrative purpose beyond “worldbuilding texture.”
